How we do it.

Greenhouse x
USB Ambi Sprint

1 Toddler Moning, Product Innovation Director at U.S. Bank, approached the Greenhouse with a potential opportunity for U.S. Bank Payments to leverage an emerging technology, ambient payments, in order to capture a new customer segment. Toddler and his team completed preliminary investigations which led to the creating of an innovation canvas, which seemed to indicate that

there might be significant value in building a payment solution that used distributed connected devices

With that 'validated hunch' in hand, Toddler approached the Greenhouse in hopes we could help him turn it into something tangible.

2 Andrew Cantrell, Leader of Applied Research at the Greenhouse, used Todder's With that 'validated hunch' in hand, Toddler approached the Greenhouse in hopes we could help him turn it into something tangible.

the Greenhouse suggested to Todder that we shoudl design a prototype which would showcae the potential for ambient technology and use that design to solicit feedback from real merchants.

3 with Todder's blessing, the Greenhouse summer interns created a

prototype that allowed merchants to easily set up a virtual farmers market

which users could navigate, shop at, and pay for goods on their phones.

The interns validated their projects with real merchants and recieved overwhelmingly positive feedback.

4 With further interest from both Todder and the Elavon product team,

The Greenhouse hosted a five-day design sprint

During the sprint, a prototype for Scoot was created, Scoot is a text and web-based application which allows consumers visiting a restaurant to set preferences while they wait, and connect a payment method, enabling them to pay when they are ready.

After receiving overwhelming feedback from real Elavon restaurant merchants, Todder and Elavon product are now ready to build a pilot, knowing that the concept is heading in the right direction and it is something their customers want.
